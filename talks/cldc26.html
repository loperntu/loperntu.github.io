Lens 1 · Statistical Memory

Culture as a Probability Distribution/Probabilistic Flattening

→ LLMs don't understand culture; they mirror the frequency of its expression
→ In a "weighted average," the loudest (most data-heavy) cultures become the ground truth
→ Minority cultures aren't simply unknown — they're force-fit into dominant frameworks; LLMs do not merely "forget" minority cultures; rather, they perform a form of "cultural colonization" by subsuming low-resource nuances under high-probability (Western) dominant equivalents.
Framing: whose language dominates the mix becomes the implicit prior in model output.
Lens 2 · Diagnostic Benchmarking of Implicit Cultural Protocols and Pragmatic Inference in LLMs

LLM Cultural Benchmarking

The Problem Facts aren't enough

Culture isn't "What is the capital of Taiwan?" — it's social coherence: pragmatics, indirectness, code-switching, face-saving.

LOBSTER · ROCLING 2025 96 IOL problems · 90+ languages:
Measures Linguistic Diversity and structural reasoning

Evaluates LLMs on linguistic reasoning with typological metadata. Goes beyond final-answer accuracy — grades reasoning chains too. Iterative MoA significantly outperforms single-pass.

IOL-like Saisiyat example
BETEL · In progress Taiwanese Culture Benchmark:
Measures Cultural Pragmatics and social context

Diagnoses cultural "traps" a global model systematically fails — pragmatic inference, code-switching, culturally situated deixis.

Cultural-Contextual Comprehension Task
Key Finding Alignment ≠ translation

Gemini-2.5-Pro scores 0.44 on LOBSTER baseline. Low-resource languages (Class 0) see near-zero E→T generation. Language family predicts failure, not script. Linguistic diversity is not just about diverse text; it is about diverse cognitive and structural frameworks that current alignment methods often fail to bridge.

Lens 3 · Shared Latent Space

The Semantic Manifold of 情 (Qing)

Utilize "情" (Qing) to illustrate curvature shifts between Classical and Modern Chinese, and the semantic divergence between Chinese embeddings and Western-centric translations (Emotion/Sentiment).
Diachronic sense tagging of 「情」 (Pre-Qin → Modern): memory capacity modulates manifold curvature — human cognition tolerates ambiguity, LLMs suppress it.
Thought · The Unalignable Issue

Some Concepts Refuse to Align

辛苦了 Pragmatic example

LLMs map this to "Good job." But in the Taiwanese / East Asian context it acknowledges shared burden — vertical social pressure, suffering-as-virtue. No English equivalent carries that weight.

阿公 Diachronic example

Beyond "Grandfather" — a social honorific for elderly males, with Hokkien-Mandarin code-switching, political-historical affect, and emotional warmth that English cannot encode in a single word.

Benchmark goal: not to fix "errors," but to identify unalignable islands — where our language refuses to be a weighted average of a global norm.
